How It Works

A real workflow from intake to verified relief delivery

01

Capture Needs

Coordinators add requests with urgency, zone, people needed, skills, and field notes so the workspace reflects real ground demand.

02

Forecast Gaps

The AidFlow layer estimates which zones may face the next shortage and suggests what kind of relief packs or support should be pre-positioned.

03

Match Responders

ResourceFlow recommends volunteers using urgency, skills, transport, experience, and zone proximity so response decisions happen faster.

04

Verify Delivery

Each high-priority request gets a QR-ready token and field trail that can support transparent relief handoff and proof-of-delivery workflows.

Problem Statement

Disaster and community response teams still rely on spreadsheets, calls, and scattered field updates.

During floods, cyclones, medical camps, shelter support drives, or urgent field visits, coordinators often do not know which request should be staffed first, which zone may face the next shortage, or which volunteers have the right skills and transport.

ResourceFlow solves this by centralizing requests, volunteers, assignments, analytics, predictive shortage signals, and delivery verification into one system that can scale from a campus event to a city-wide relief operation.

AidFlow AI Layer

Predictive disaster allocation inside the same prototype

Shortage Forecasting

The app can estimate which high-risk zones are likely to face the next staffing or supply shortfall so teams can pre-position aid.

Verified Delivery

Each request can surface a QR-ready delivery token and status trail so field teams have a simple chain of custody for relief packs.

Earth Engine Ready

The insight layer is structured so satellite, weather, or flood-risk signals can later be added through Google Earth Engine and Gemini.

Proactive Response

Instead of reacting only after requests pile up, coordinators can use forecast, fairness, and risk signals to intervene earlier.
